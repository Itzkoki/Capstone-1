    Please read and agree to both notices below before starting your intake form.

    Data Privacy Act Consent

    In compliance with the Data Privacy Act of 2012 (Republic Act No. 10173), its Implementing Rules and Regulations, and applicable issuances of the National Privacy Commission, Barcarse Psychological Services collects and processes your personal and sensitive personal information for the following purposes:

    • To assess your needs, schedule appointments, and deliver counseling and psychological assessment services.
    • To create and maintain your clinical and case records.
    • To contact you and, where necessary, your emergency contact or guardian.
    • To comply with legal, regulatory, and professional obligations.

    Your information is kept strictly confidential, stored securely, and is accessed only by authorized personnel. It will not be shared with third parties without your consent, except as required or permitted by law. You have the right to access, correct, and object to the processing of your data, and to withdraw this consent at any time, subject to legal and clinical limitations.

    Philippine Code of Ethics for Psychologists

    Barcarse Psychological Services adheres to the Code of Ethics for Philippine Psychologists of the Psychological Association of the Philippines (PAP) and the Philippine Psychology Act of 2009 (Republic Act No. 10029). In rendering counseling and psychotherapy services, our practitioners are guided by the following core principles:

    • Respect for the Dignity of Persons and Peoples — we uphold your worth, rights, autonomy, and informed consent throughout your care.
    • Competence — services are provided only within our practitioners' areas of training, competence, and licensure.
    • Responsibility — we are accountable for the appropriate, caring, and professional conduct of every session.
    • Integrity — we provide honest, objective, and fair professional services, free from conflicts of interest.
    • Confidentiality — information shared in counseling is disclosed only with your consent or as required by law and professional standards.

    I understand the professional and ethical standards under which counseling and psychotherapy are provided, including the limits of confidentiality where disclosure is required or permitted by law.
